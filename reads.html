My Reading Log

Gödel's Proof (Revised Edition)
Ernest Bafel, James R. Newman, Dougls R. Hofstadter
Read: March 2026
Rating 8 / 10
I started reading this after receiving the results to my first ever logic module, it's safe to say it convinced me to stay on track with formal logic in philosophy.
Number Theory
George E. Andrews
Read: August 2025
Rating 5 / 10

Introduction to Mathematical Philosophy
Bertrand Russell
Read: April 2024
Rating 7 / 10
This is the book that made me choose my degree so it definitely holds alot of value, I must admit I didn't understand alot of the logical notation used on my first read.
Zero: The Biography of a Dangerous Idea
Charles Seife
Read: 2020
Rating 8 / 10
When I was younger this was a book I always went back to, and one of the only books I've read to date that focuses on the history of mathematics more than rigour, which definitely made it more accessible for me at the age of 13 when I first read it.
