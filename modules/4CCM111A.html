4CCM111A

Calculus I

Level 4 Year 1, Semester 1
Module Description Overview
Aims: The aim of the module is to review and enhance aspects of pre-university mathematics in order to foster genuine confidence and fluency with the material. This will help provide a firm grasp of basic ideas thus allowing concentration on the many new and often abstract concepts that will be introduced in various modules throughout the academic programme.
Syllabus: Injective, subjective and bijective functions; trigonometric functions; hyperbolic functions; inverse functions; the logarithm and exponential functions; limits; differentiation from first principles; integration from first principles; series; Taylor's theorem; l'Hopital's rule; and basic Python programming.
Assessment & Examination Examined
This module, alike to many other Level 4 Modules, includes two class tests that will be taken within term time, the first one covers content from Weeks 1-5, whilst the second covers all the Python content on the course. The final exam for this module is sat in January (Assessment Period 1).
